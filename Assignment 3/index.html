CLOWNFISH

Did you know that clownfish is the species of Nemo and Marlin?

Clownfish is also called anemonefish. There are many types of clownfish and Nemo and Marlin are categorized as False Clownfish. Most false clownfish are orange with white bands on their head and body and is outlined in black. They are also one of the most beautiful species hence they are a popular aquarium pet.

Clownfish live in the coral reefs off the coasts of Australia and Southeast Asia. They are found mainly around anemones, a kind of creature that uses its tentacles to attract food. Despite the danger of anemones, clownfish developed immunity to the toxins of anemones. Amazingly, clownfish and anemone work together. Clownfish gives food to the anemone and then the anemone will remove the parasites.

So how do they communicate? Clownfish communicate by making popping and clicking noises.

Another weird, but amazing fact about clownfish is that they are hermaphrodites. They have the ability to turn into female.

Common Name: Clownfish
Type: Fish
Diet: Carnivore
Group Name: School
Average Life Span in the Wild: 6 to 10 years
Size: 4.3 inches
